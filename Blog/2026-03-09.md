<h1>Implemting the more Advanced Mechanics</h1>

During this session we implemented the complex physics of the bounce. The normal force which is an approximation of a mass-spring-damper system and the forces applied by the ball either sliding or gripping. The later mentioned is the force we used to calculate the torque of the ball during contact. After calculating the torque we used the Euler method to calculate the new angular velocity since it is impacted during contact with the ground.

Additionally we added multiple tennis balls in the scene so that we could visualize them at the same time. We also changed the color of each trail depending on the type of spin it had.
