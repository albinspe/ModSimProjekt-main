<h1> Implementing some basic mechanics </h1>

During this session we wanted to implement the mechanics using an integration method and for this we chose Euler's method. We began by removing the built-in mechanics that come from the RigidBody component on our sphere. We did this by deactivating built-in gravity and activated the use kinematics option.

To make the trajectory of the ball easier to follow, since the ball is quite small in comparison to the court, we added a trail that is built-in in Unity.

To simulate the mechanics we started with an initial velocity and added a constant gravitational force. We then did the Euler method in the loop that updates every physics frame by calculating the acceleration from the total force on the sphere, calculating the velocity by mulitplying the acceleration by a small time step, and calculating the new position from adding the velocity multiplied by the time step to the old position.

We counted the bounces by tracking the y-position of the sphere and stopped the simulation at the second contact with the ground.

Finally we implemented the aerodynamic forces (drag and lift) from the formulas we found in an earlier study that depend on the velocity and angular velocity of the sphere. To make this an easy experience we added a drop-down menu in the inspector for the sphere were we can choose the type of spin we want in the simulation.
